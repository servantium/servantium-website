## What PSA was built for

Professional Services Automation tools (Kantata, Certinia, Changepoint, Salesforce PSA) were designed in the 2000s to solve one problem: get timesheets into invoices with utilization reporting on top. That was a big deal at the time.

## What changed

Modern services firms don't just need automation. They need a system that:
- Scopes engagements faster and more accurately
- Prices fixed-fee work with historical confidence
- Runs delivery with live margin visibility
- Learns from every engagement and feeds it into the next

That's a Professional Services OS, not a PSA.

## The difference in practice

| Stage | PSA | Professional Services OS |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scope | External tool or Word doc | Native, AI-drafted from conversation |
| Price | Rate card lookup | Historical anchoring + margin modeling |
| Deliver | Timesheet + invoice | Live margin, scope, and resource visibility |
| Learn | Nothing | Every engagement trains the next |

## When PSA is still right

Large firms with mature PMOs, heavy ERP integrations, and compliance requirements often need a full-blown PSA. Everyone else has outgrown it.
