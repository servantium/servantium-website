## Building a quote

Navigate to the **Quotes** tab within any engagement to start pricing.

### Adding groups

Groups organize your quote into logical sections. Common patterns:

- **By phase:** Discovery, Implementation, Training & Rollout
- **By workstream:** Technical, Change Management, Project Management
- **By deliverable:** Platform Setup, Data Migration, Custom Development

<Steps>

1. Click **Add Group** in the quote toolbar.
2. Name the group (e.g., "Discovery & Requirements").
3. Add line items within the group.

</Steps>

### Line item types

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Resource** | A person/role with hours, rate, and markup | "Lead Consultant — 40 hours @ $250" |
| **Material** | A tangible cost (licenses, hardware, etc.) | "Training Materials — $3,500" |
| **Fixed Fee** | A flat amount not tied to hours | "Project Management Fee — $10,000" |

### Markup and margin

Each line item and group can have its own **markup percentage**. Servantium calculates:

- **Cost** — Your actual cost (hours × rate, or material cost)
- **Markup** — Applied percentage on top of cost
- **Total** — What the client pays

The **estimate total bar** at the bottom shows aggregate cost, average markup, and total estimate across all groups.

<Aside type="tip">
Set default markup percentages per engagement type. A standard implementation might default to 40% while a strategic advisory engagement defaults to 60%.
</Aside>

## Quote templates

For recurring engagement types, save a quote as a **template**. Templates encode:

- Standard group structure
- Default line items and roles
- Typical quantities and rates
- Target markup percentages

When you create a new engagement from a template, the quote pre-populates — you adjust rather than build from scratch.
